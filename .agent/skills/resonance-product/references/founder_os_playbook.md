# Founder OS Playbook: The Entrepreneurial Operating System (EOS)

The Founder OS Playbook provides the operational framework for running a high-accountability organization. It is based on the Level 10 (L10) meeting structure and the IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve) protocol.

## 1. The L10 Meeting Structure (90 Minutes)
The L10 is the weekly leadership meeting that keeps the team aligned, accountable, and solving problems. It follows a strict 7-section agenda with time boxes.

| # | Section | Time | Purpose |
|---|---------|------|---------|
| 1 | Segue | 5 min | Personal/professional good news |
| 2 | Scorecard | 5 min | Review weekly numbers. Note off-track items for Issues list. |
| 3 | Rock Review | 5 min | Status of quarterly Rocks (on/off track). |
| 4 | Headlines | 5 min | Customer and employee news. |
| 5 | To-Do Review | 5 min | Check last week's To-Dos. Target: 90%+ completion. |
| 6 | **IDS** | 60 min | Identify, Discuss, Solve top issues. |
| 7 | Conclude | 5 min | New To-Dos, cascading messages, meeting rating (1-10). |

## 2. The IDS Protocol (Identify, Discuss, Solve)
This is the core of the L10. Do not skip or rush this section.

### Step 1: Build the Issues List
Collect issues from:
- Off-track Scorecard metrics.
- Off-track Rocks.
- Open organizational issues.
- Headlines/New issues raised by attendees.

### Step 2: Prioritize (Top 3)
List all issues and force the team to pick the **top 3** most important ones. These get solved today. The rest roll over to next week.

### Step 3: Execute IDS (For each of the Top 3)
1. **Identify**: What is the real issue? Dig past symptoms. Use the "5 Whys" if the root cause isn't obvious. The stated problem is rarely the root problem.
2. **Discuss**: Each person shares their perspective. Stay focused — no tangents, no solving yet. Ensure everyone is heard.
3. **Solve**: What is the definitive action? Create at least one To-Do with a clear **Owner** and **Due Date**. Cross-reference the Accountability Chart to validate that the To-Do owner matches the seat responsible for that area.

## 3. Core Guardrails
- **Respect Time Boxes**: Do not let any section (especially IDS) expand beyond its allocation without acknowledging the trade-off.
- **Data over Narrative**: Scorecard and Rock Review must use actual data. If metrics are missing, that is an issue in itself.
- **No Floating To-Dos**: Every action item must have a singular owner. "The team" is not an owner.
- **Accountability over Guilt**: If To-Do completion is below 90%, it is data, not a guilt trip. Ask why the system failed, not why the person failed.
