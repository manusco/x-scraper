# Product-Market Fit (PMF) Diagnostics

This protocol provides the framework to determine whether a product has achieved Product-Market Fit (PMF) and, if not, what strategic pivot is required. PMF is not binary—it is a spectrum, and it can be lost.

## 1. The Sean Ellis Test (The 40% Rule)
The most objective measure of PMF is the Sean Ellis survey.

**The Core Question:**
*"How would you feel if you could no longer use [Product]?"*
- Very disappointed
- Somewhat disappointed
- Not disappointed
- N/A — I no longer use it

**The Benchmark:**
If **40% or more** of respondents answer "Very disappointed", you have strong signal for Product-Market Fit. If it is below 40%, you do not have PMF and should focus on product iterations before scaling acquisition.

## 2. Signal Assessment

### Strong PMF Signals
- High retention and repeat usage.
- Organic, word-of-mouth growth.
- Pull from customers (they are demanding the product, you are struggling to keep up).

### Weak PMF Signals
- High churn rate.
- Feature requests that require changing the core architecture or value proposition.
- Extreme price sensitivity.

### No PMF Signals
- Growth is driven *exclusively* by paid acquisition.
- Low engagement metrics despite sign-ups.
- High support volume for fundamental "how does this help me?" questions.

## 3. The Pivot Framework
If PMF is not achieved, evaluate these strategic pivots rather than blindly building more features:

- **Zoom In**: A single feature becomes the entire product. (Double down on what users love most).
- **Zoom Out**: The current product becomes just one feature of a larger platform.
- **Customer Pivot**: The product stays the same, but the target audience changes.
- **Need Pivot**: The target audience stays the same, but you solve a different problem for them.
- **Channel Pivot**: The product and audience stay the same, but the distribution mechanism changes.

## 4. Execution Rules
- Do not declare PMF based on vanity metrics (signups, downloads, raw traffic).
- Be ruthlessly honest. If the data suggests a pivot is needed, present that recommendation clearly.
- PMF is a prerequisite for scaling. Do not recommend growth marketing spend until the Sean Ellis test benchmark is met.
