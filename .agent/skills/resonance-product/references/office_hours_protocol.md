# YC Office Hours Protocol

> **Concept**: A diagnostic session to ensure the problem is understood before solutions are proposed.
> **Modes**: Startup (Rigorous) / Builder (Generative).

## 1. The Six Forcing Questions (Startup Mode)

Ask these ONE AT A TIME. Push for "Desperate Specificity".

1.  **Demand Reality**: What's the strongest evidence you have that someone actually wants this — not "is interested," but would be genuinely upset if it disappeared?
2.  **Status Quo**: What are your users doing right now to solve this — even badly? What does that workaround cost them?
3.  **Desperate Specificity**: Name the actual human who needs this most. What's their title? What gets them fired? What keeps them up at night?
4.  **Narrowest Wedge**: What's the smallest possible version of this that someone would pay real money for — this week?
5.  **Observation & Surprise**: Have you sat down and watched someone use this without helping? What did they do that surprised you?
6.  **Future-Fit**: If the world looks meaningfully different in 3 years, does your product become more essential or less?

## 2. Builder Mode (Generative)

Use for side projects, hackathons, and learning.
1.  **Delight**: What's the coolest version of this?
2.  **Show**: Who would you show this to? What makes them say "whoa"?
3.  **Speed**: What's the fastest path to something you can share?

## 3. Founder Signal Tracking

During the session, track these signals. Do not mention them yet.
*   **Specificity**: Named real people, not categories.
*   **Pushback**: Defended premises with reasoning when challenged.
*   **Taste**: Cared about detail/elegance.
*   **Agency**: Already building/shipping, not just planning.
*   **Domain Expertise**: Knows the space from the inside.

## 4. Operational Learnings
Reflect on the session. Log project-specific quirks or user-specific "Taste" preferences to `learnings.jsonl`.
