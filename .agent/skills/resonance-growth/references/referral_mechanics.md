# Referral Mechanics & Viral Loop Design

> **Objective**: Engineer referral systems that compound. K > 1 means organic growth.

## 1. The Viral Coefficient (K-Factor)

```
K = (Invitations per user) × (Conversion rate of invitations)
```

- **K > 1**: Viral growth (each user brings more than one new user).
- **K = 0.5-1.0**: Referral-assisted growth (supplements other channels).
- **K < 0.5**: Referral program exists but doesn't move the needle.

**Reality check**: Pure K > 1 is rare. K = 0.3-0.7 is typical for strong referral programs. The goal is compounding, not replacement of paid acquisition.

## 2. Referral Loop Design

### The Loop Structure
```
User gets value → Prompted to share → Friend receives invite →
Friend signs up → Friend gets value → Prompted to share → ...
```

### Loop Mechanics That Work

| Mechanic | How It Works | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Give/Get** | Both sides rewarded | Dropbox: Give 500MB, Get 500MB |
| **Status** | Referrer earns visible status | Waitlist position jumps |
| **Access** | Unlock features by referring | "Invite 3 friends to unlock Pro" |
| **Credit** | Account credit for referrals | $10 credit per referral |
| **Exclusive** | Invite-only creates scarcity | Superhuman, Clubhouse |

### Choosing the Right Incentive

| Product Type | Best Incentive | Why |
|-------------|---------------|-----|
| Free product | Storage/features/credits | No cash exchanged; value-aligned |
| Paid SaaS | Account credit or free months | Reduces churn + drives referrals |
| Marketplace | Credit for both sides | Both buyer + seller benefit |
| Premium product | Cash/gift cards | High-ticket = high reward needed |

## 3. Referral Program Architecture

### The 3 Triggers (When to Prompt)
1. **Activation milestone**: User just achieved their first success → high motivation to share.
2. **Value moment**: User receives a delightful outcome → emotional peak (Peak-End Rule).
3. **Routine touchpoint**: Monthly summary email, account dashboard → consistent reminder.

**Anti-pattern**: Prompting at signup (before value delivered) = spam. Users share after they're impressed, not before.

### Friction Reduction
- **One-click sharing**: Pre-populated message + unique referral link.
- **Multi-channel**: Email, social, direct link, QR code.
- **Tracking visibility**: Show referral status ("3 friends signed up, 1 pending").
- **Instant rewards**: Don't make them wait 30 days for credit.

## 4. Measuring Referral Health

| Metric | What It Tells You | Target |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| Referral participation rate | % of users who share | >15% |
| Referral conversion rate | % of invitees who sign up | >10% |
| Time to first referral | How quickly users share | <7 days |
| Referral quality | LTV of referred vs non-referred users | Referred ≥ organic |
| K-factor | Overall viral coefficient | >0.3 |

## 5. Common Failures

1. **Invisible program**: Buried in settings → no one finds it. Surface it post-activation.
2. **Weak incentive**: $1 credit for a $100/mo product → not motivating.
3. **Complex rules**: "Refer 5 friends who each spend $50 in 30 days" → too much friction.
4. **No tracking**: Users can't see if their referrals converted → lose trust.
5. **Gaming susceptibility**: Self-referral, fake accounts → require email verification + activity threshold.
