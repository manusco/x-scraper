# Content Strategy Protocol

> **Goal**: Plan content that drives traffic, builds authority, and generates leads by being searchable, shareable, or both.

## 1. Searchable vs Shareable

Every piece of content must be searchable, shareable, or both. Prioritize in that order — search traffic is the foundation.

| Type | Purpose | Optimized For | Examples |
|------|---------|--------------|---------|
| **Searchable** | Capture existing demand | People actively looking for answers | How-to guides, tutorials, comparison pages |
| **Shareable** | Create demand | Spreading ideas, getting people talking | Original research, hot takes, case studies |
| **Both** | Rare but powerful | Search + social distribution | Data-driven guides, definitive resources |

### When Writing Searchable Content
- Target a specific keyword or question.
- Match search intent exactly — answer what the searcher wants.
- Structure with headings that mirror search patterns.
- Provide comprehensive coverage (don't leave questions unanswered).
- Optimize for AI/LLM discovery: clear positioning, structured content, self-contained answer blocks.

### When Writing Shareable Content
- Lead with a novel insight, original data, or counterintuitive take.
- Challenge conventional wisdom with well-reasoned arguments.
- Tell stories that make people feel something.
- Create content people share to look smart or help others.

## 2. Content Pillars

Content pillars are 3-5 core topics your brand will own. Each pillar spawns a cluster of related content.

### How to Identify Pillars
1. **Product-led**: What problems does your product solve?
2. **Audience-led**: What does your ICP need to learn?
3. **Search-led**: What topics have volume in your space?
4. **Competitor-led**: What are competitors ranking for that you aren't?

### Pillar Structure
```
Pillar Topic (Hub)
├── Subtopic Cluster 1
│   ├── Article A
│   └── Article B
├── Subtopic Cluster 2
│   ├── Article C
│   └── Article D
└── Subtopic Cluster 3
    ├── Article E
    └── Article F
```

**Good pillars should:**
- Align with your product/service
- Match what your audience cares about
- Have search volume and/or social interest
- Be broad enough for multiple subtopics

## 3. Keyword Research by Buyer Stage

Map content to the buyer's journey:

| Stage | Keyword Modifiers | Content Intent |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| **Awareness** | "what is," "how to," "guide to" | Educate, build trust |
| **Consideration** | "best," "vs," "alternatives," "comparison" | Help evaluate |
| **Decision** | "pricing," "reviews," "demo," "trial" | Convert |
| **Implementation** | "templates," "tutorial," "how to use" | Activate, retain |

## 4. Content Types

### Searchable Types
- **Use-case content**: [Persona] + [use-case] (long-tail keywords).
- **Hub-and-spoke**: Comprehensive hub page + subtopic spokes with interlinking.
- **Template libraries**: High-intent keywords + product adoption.

### Shareable Types
- **Thought leadership**: Name concepts everyone feels but hasn't articulated.
- **Data-driven content**: Original research, product data analysis, public data insights.
- **Case studies**: Challenge → Solution → Results → Key learnings.
- **Meta content**: Behind-the-scenes transparency ("How We Got Our First $5k MRR").

## 5. Content Ideation Sources

| Source | What to Extract |
|--------|----------------|
| **Keyword data** | Topic clusters, buyer stage, quick wins (low competition + decent volume) |
| **Sales calls** | Questions, objections, pain points, customer language |
| **Support tickets** | Repeated questions, frustrations, feature requests |
| **Forums** (Reddit, HN, Quora) | FAQs, misconceptions, debates, terminology |
| **Competitor content** | Topics they cover well, gaps they miss, outdated content to improve |
| **Customer surveys** | Open-ended themes, resource requests, format preferences |

## 6. Prioritizing Content Ideas

Score each idea on four factors:

| Factor | Weight | Questions |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Customer Impact** | 40% | How frequently mentioned? How emotionally charged? |
| **Content-Market Fit** | 30% | Does it align with product? Can we offer unique insights? |
| **Search Potential** | 20% | Volume? Competition? Growing or declining? |
| **Resource Requirements** | 10% | Do we have expertise? What assets are needed? |

## 7. Output Format

When creating a content strategy, deliver:
1. **Content Pillars**: 3-5 pillars with rationale and subtopic clusters.
2. **Priority Topics**: Ranked list with keyword, buyer stage, content type, and rationale.
3. **Topic Cluster Map**: Visual representation of how content interconnects.
